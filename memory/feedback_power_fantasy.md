---
name: Power fantasy framing
description: Player is the threat, not the threatened — don't design around the player's safety
type: feedback
---

The player is an alien invader and is always the aggressor. Design decisions should prioritize power-fantasy feel over the player's defensive concerns.

**Why:** User explicitly pushed back on my caution about "losing peripheral awareness of threats" when discussing a closer zoom level. Their reply: "we dont care about threats, we are the threat." The aesthetic is predator, not prey.

**How to apply:**
- When weighing tradeoffs, don't hedge with "but the player might get flanked / ambushed / overwhelmed" — those are upsides, not downsides, because the player is strong.
- Prefer cinematic/intimate framing (tighter zoom, big creature scale, dramatic effects) over wide tactical views.
- Don't add UI clutter like threat indicators, minimap enemy pings, "incoming attack" warnings unless asked.
- If a feature makes the player feel like a big scary thing stomping around, lean into it. Earthlings/military exist to panic and die, not to be meaningfully dangerous.
